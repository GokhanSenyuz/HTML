Brian Morgan Palmer

My first passion was general aviation and I started flying small planes when I was sixteen. After high school, I attended Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University in Prescott, Arizona for 2 years. Although I didn't have the resources to complete the program, I did earn a Commercial / Instrument license and have over 400 hours of flight time.

When I returned to New London, New Hampshire, I attended the University of New Hampshire for Computer Science. While in school, I lived at home and worked at Campion's (Dartmouth Outfitter) and for my aunt’s store, the Hanover Hardware Store.

My first position was with Lockheed-Martin in Nashua NH, where I had numerous software development roles and completed a Master of Computer Science at Boston University. I've always liked the New Hampshire Seacoast, so after 5 years of Department of Defense work, I took a position at Cabletron Systems in Durham, NH, writing internet services.

I've spent the majority of my profession career developing value-added services for the internet (network management, web platforms, VoIP, QOS, etc.), but for the last 6 years I've been more involved with cloud services. Most recently, I’ve worked in the Biotechology sector, helping researchers develop biostatistical platforms for big genomic data.

When I'm not busy being a student of science, I enjoy spending time with my three children and almost everything outside. I like to jog, bike, hike, sail and I'm an avid skier (water & snow).
