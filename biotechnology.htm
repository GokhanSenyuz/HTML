Biotechnology

My first role in the field of Biotechnology was working on a project to collect a million DNA samples from veterans for the United States Department of Veterans Affairs. The Boston VA Research Institute (BVARI), located at the Jamaica Plain Health Care Facility, was looking for an engineer who had experience in data extraction and normalization from disparate computer systems.

While most of my day-to-day work did not involve biology, I did get experience working with researchers, as well as an introduction to the many legal issues surrounding the collection of human samples. I was contracted to create a master database of possible research candidates (veterans) and develop an application to solicit their participation in the program - Million Veteran Program (www.research.va.gov/mvp).

Leveraging my limited experience in the field, I began to target academic institutions performing biotechnology research. My next role was as a Scientific System Administrator for a lab at the Harvard School of Public Health (HSPH) focusing on computational models for functional genomics in microbial communities.

In this role, I was much more involved with the researchers and provided all the computational support for a large grant given to the HSPH from the Cohn's and Colitis Foundation. I was also charged with managing the bio-statistical systems for the research team. I worked closely with researchers and statisticians from all over the world. More information is available on the website (www.huttenhower.org).

This is when I knew that this is what I wanted to do for the rest of my professional career. Not only did I get to work with the most intelligent people / researchers I had ever met, but the technological challenges were extremely exciting and rewarding.

This fall I will be attending the BioScience Academy at Boston University Medical School full-time on a one-year scholarship. I look forward to the challenges ahead, both academic and professional.
